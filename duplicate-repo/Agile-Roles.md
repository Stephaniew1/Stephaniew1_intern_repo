# Research & Learn
### What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?
* Developers - These are team members with responsibilities not limited to product development. They run sprints as per the requirements provided by the product owner and coordinated by the scrum master. 
* Quality Assurannce (QA) - collaborate with developers and product managers to help define the deliverables, perform continuous testing and provide rapid feedback. 
* Product Manager (PM) -They manage the release cycle planning to ensure that the development team can deliver valuable updated product iterations on a continuous basis. 
* Agile Project Manager (Agile PM) - They not only manage the project but also focus on promoting effective collaboration and facilitating quick adaptation to serve the customer’s needs. They define the deliverables, plan each development sprint and ensure that all team members are aligned through agile principles. 
* Stakeholders – They provide feedback to the team and assist with end user expectations and product development 

### How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum?
Scrum and Kanban both aim to improve efficiency and adaptability however, they differ in structure, delivery and cadence. Scrum utilises fixed-length sprints and defined roles while Kanban focuses on continuous flow.
