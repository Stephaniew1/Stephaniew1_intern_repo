
# Research & Learn
### How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
A Kanban board is a visual management tool used to map out the team’s workflow thus enabling members to see the status of every task. It manages workflow by visualizing work, using a pull system, workflow stage and implementing restriction identification. 


### What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent? (e.g., Backlog, In Progress, Blocked, Done)
The columns represent the steps a task takes from beginning to end. These columns include:
* Backlog – A repository of incomplete tasks
* Prioritized – These are tasks that take precedence and are ready to be completed.
* In Progress – Tasks that are being actively completed
* Blocked - Tasks that cannot move forward/completed due to dependency or an external issue
* Done – Completed tasks

### How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks move from left to right as work is completed. It is expected that team members update their own task so that the board can reflect it in real-time. 

### What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
The benefits of limiting work in progress is higher output, lower overload and improved quality as one is able to completely focus on the task at hand.


# Reflections
### How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?
Kanban helps manage priorities and avoid overload through limiting work in progress. This is done by capping active tasks and requiring team members to finish one item before pulling another.
