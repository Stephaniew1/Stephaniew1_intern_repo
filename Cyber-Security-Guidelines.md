# My Reflections
1. What security measures do you currently follow, and where can you improve?
I do not use public Wi-Fi and text-based authenticators as I am aware of the vulnerabilities my accounts and devices are exposed to. One area of improvement could be employing the use of password managers. 

2. How can you make secure behaviour a habit rather than an afterthought?
A secure behaviour is formed by making it a routine, i.e., locking the computer before leaving it unattended to prevent unwanted/unauthorized users from gaining access to sensitive information. Additionally, enabling 2FA where possible ensures that all passwords and accounts are encrypted and secure. 

3. What steps will you take to ensure your passwords and accounts are secure?
In addition to using various 2FA methods, I would adopt utilising passkeys and in-app authenticators as opposed to text-based 2FA. Moreover, I would use a password manager and store backup codes in a safe place. 

4. What would you do if you suspected a security breach or suspicious activity on your account?
In the event of a suspected security breach, I would change passwords immediately, scan the device for malware and monitor the suspected account.
