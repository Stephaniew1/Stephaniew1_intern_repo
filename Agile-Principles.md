
# Research & Learn
### What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
##### Agile is described as an iterative, flexible project management approach that focuses on continuous improvement, collaboration and delivery. While traditional project management follows a linear, rigid and sequential approach that adapts to change by incorporating stakeholder feedback and empowering self-organizing teams

### What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)
##### The core values and principles of Agile are, individuals and interactions over process and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation, customer collaboration over contract negotiation and responding to change over following a plan. 

### What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
##### Scrum follows fixed-length sprints (iterations) with defined roles to deliver working software gradually. This method is ideal for projects where the priorities change weekly. Whereas Kanban applies a continuous flow by using Work-in-progress (WIP) limits to manage output without set timeboxes. It is used by mature teams working on continuous but unpredictable work that needs immediate release. 

### Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
##### Focus Bear tends to lean towards Kanban as its core goal is to provide flexible, continuous workflow that adapts to changing schedules rather than rigid time-boxed sprints.
